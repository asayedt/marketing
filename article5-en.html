What Are Pelvic Adhesions?

Pelvic adhesions are bands of scar tissue that form inside the pelvic area, causing organs to stick to each other or to the pelvic wall. This can affect the uterus, ovaries, bladder, or fallopian tubes.

These adhesions develop due to inflammation or injury and may cause several symptoms, including:

  • Pain during intercourse.
  • Chronic pelvic pain.
  • Difficulty conceiving (infertility).
  • Bowel or bladder problems.

Treatment of Pelvic Adhesions

The treatment of pelvic adhesions depends on the severity of the condition and how it affects the organs.

Non-surgical treatments:

Laparoscopic surgery expert Dr. Ayman Tahir recommends managing pelvic adhesions through Kegel exercises to strengthen the pelvic muscles, along with lifestyle modifications such as weight loss and constipation treatment.

Surgical treatment:

Surgery is only considered if the adhesions cause significant pain or health issues. The procedure involves removing the scar tissue, and Dr. Ayman Tahir advises performing this operation using laparoscopy for optimal results with minimal complications.

Do Pelvic Adhesions Prevent Pregnancy?

Yes, pelvic adhesions can affect fertility by restricting the movement of reproductive organs, making conception more difficult. These adhesions may also block the passage of eggs through the fallopian tubes, reducing the chances of fertilization.
